Description

Andrew Szigethy is an Engineering Manager, SWE & Architecture who specializes in software platforms, product architecture, automation, observability, delivery systems, and the organizational habits that make technical teams more effective. His career has been shaped by environments where reliability, customer impact, and practical execution all mattered at once.

Professional narrative

He began in production-heavy work, learned the business from the inside, and built technical fluency through careful operations, data automation, and incident awareness. Over time, that practical foundation expanded into backend engineering, systems design, team leadership, and organization-level work on standards, review rigor, onboarding, and delivery governance.

What makes his profile unusual is the mix. He is not a narrow operational specialist, and he is not a manager who stands apart from the technical work. He sits where software, architecture, and execution discipline meet. That allows him to connect engineering decisions to business outcomes in a way that is direct, credible, and useful.

Current focus

Andrew is at his best in roles that require platform thinking, strong delivery systems, product judgment, and calm leadership under pressure. He helps organizations mature both the software itself and the structure around the software.

That includes architecture tradeoffs, engineering standards, onboarding systems, quality review habits, and the connective work between teams, stakeholders, and long-term product direction.

Leadership philosophy

Clarity over noise

Andrew prefers to make the right answer easier to reach rather than simply asserting authority. In practice, that means better definitions, clearer standards, tighter feedback loops, and more explicit ownership.

Discipline that helps delivery

He believes engineering organizations do their best work when quality, review, and observability are treated as part of the product. The goal is disciplined execution that increases speed instead of fighting it.

Operating principles

Turn ambiguity into executable plans
Use standards to reduce friction
Connect technical work to business value
Protect the team while raising the bar
Build mechanisms that outlast the moment

Interests

Andrew is an electronics and robotics hobbyist with a strong interest in STEM education and communication. He cares about helping people reach the right answer, not just hearing a quick answer. He has also spoken with high school math classes, which reflects the same instinct he brings to leadership: make complicated things clearer, more approachable, and more useful.

His style is calm, fair, and direct. He values curiosity, motivation, and wisdom. When the work is hard, he is usually most helpful as the person who can steady the conversation, surface the tradeoffs, and keep the team moving toward a sound decision.

Where he fits best

Andrew is especially effective in organizations that need to scale software capability, improve engineering maturity, and create better systems around delivery. He is well suited to platform work, architecture leadership, product engineering, and cross-functional environments where technical quality and commercial value need to move together.
